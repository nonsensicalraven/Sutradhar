Masks,Puppetry, and Performing Crafts

Masks in Indian performance traditions—such as Chhau, Theyyam, and Himalayan Cham are crafted from clay, wood, bamboo, or papier-mâché to embody deities, spirits, and heroes in ritual theatre. Puppetry spans string (Kathputli), shadow (Tholu Bommalata, Togalu Gombeyaata), and glove/rod forms, with artisans making masks, costumes, and props that animate epics and folklore on stage.

Kathputli (Rajasthan)

String puppetry, folk epics and social tales.
Togalu Gombeyaata (Karnataka)

Leather shadow puppetry.

Tholu Bommalata (Andhra Pradesh)

Large leather shadow puppets, Ramayana/Mahabharata.

West Bengal, Jharkhand, Odisha

Painted masks for martial dance-drama.

Theyyam (Kerala):

Elaborate ritual performance with body paint, headgear.

Yakshagana (Karnataka)

Dance-drama with stylized costumes and makeup.
