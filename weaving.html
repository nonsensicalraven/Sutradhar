Weaving and Loom Heritage

India’s weaving and loom heritage spans millennia, from fine cotton muslins and khadi to luxuriant silks like Kanchipuram, Banarasi, Paithani, and rich woollens such as Kani and Kullu shawls. Distinct regional looms and techniques—ikat (Pochampally, Patola), jamdani, chanderi, and durries—create signature textures, borders, and motifs, preserving living traditions of handloom craftsmanship.

Banarasi (Uttar Pradesh)

Silk brocades with zari.

Kanchipuram (Tamil Nadu)

Temple-bordered silks with korvai joins.

Paithani (Maharashtra)

Peacock and floral pallus with real zari.

Baluchari (West Bengal)

Narrative pallus with mythological scenes.

Pashmina (Kashmir)

Fine cashmere shawls with kani or sozni work.
