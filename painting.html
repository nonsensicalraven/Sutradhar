Painting and scrolls

Indian painting spans from prehistoric rock art and Ajanta murals to Mughalâ€“Rajput miniatures and living folk styles, unified by vivid color, symbolism, and storytelling. Scroll traditions narrate epics and local lore across long cloth or paper with bold outlines and earthy palettes.

Madhubani(Mithila, Bihar/Nepal)

Geometric patterns, natural dyes, mythological themes.
Warli art(Maharashtra)

Minimalist white-on-ochre figures, community life, Tarpa dance motifs.

Pattachitra (Odisha, West Bengal)

Cloth-based scrolls, bold lines, Jagannath and Vaishnav themes.

Phad(Rajasthan)

Large narrative scrolls of Pabuji/Devnarayan, used in itinerant performances.

kalamkari(Andhra Pradesh, Telangana)

Pen-drawn textiles with natural dyes; Machilipatnam and Srikalahasti styles.

Kalighat(Kolkata, West Bengal)

19th-century bold, satirical watercolors on paper, gods and social themes.

Cheriyal Scrolls (Telangana)

Narrative scrolls with red backgrounds, folk epics and village lore.

Gond (Madhya Pradesh)

Tribal painting with signature dashes/dots, nature and animal spirits.

Bhil (Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Rajasthan)

Dotted patterns, folklore and harvest rituals.

Pithora (Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh)

Ritual wall paintings by Rathwa/Bhil communities.

Saura(Odisha)

Tribal wall paintings with stick figures and sacred motifs.

Tanjore (Tamil Nadu)

Panel paintings with gold leaf, relief work, Bhakti subjects.

Kerala Mural (Kerala)

Temple murals, natural pigments, larger-than-life deities.

Minature Schools(Rajasthan, Pahari, Mughal, Deccani)

Intricate courtly paintings on paper.

Thangka (Himalayan regions)

Buddhist scrolls, deities and mandalas.

Jadopatia/Jadupatua (Jharkhand)

Santhal narrative scrolls on paper or cloth

chitrakathi(Maharashtra):

Storytelling pictures used with oral narration.

Aipan/Aripan(Uttarakhand/Bihar)

Ritual floor and wall designs with rice paste/ochre.
