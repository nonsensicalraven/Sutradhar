Textile, Dyeing and Printing Arts

Indian textiles excel in weaving, dyeing, printing, and embroidery, from fine cottons and silks to regionally distinct brocades, jamdani, and shawls. Resist-dye and print traditions—bandhani, leheriya, ajrakh, kalamkari, ikat—create signature motifs with natural dyes, hand-blocks, and yarn-dye techniques.

Ajrakh (Kutch, Gujarat, Sindh)

Block-printed indigo-madder textiles with geometric repeats.
Bagh Printing (Madhya Pradesh)

Vegetable-dyed block prints with floral and geometric motifs.
Ikat/Patola (Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat)

Resist-dyed yarns forming precise patterns.
Chikankari (Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh)

Delicate whitework embroidery.
Kantha (Bengal)

Running-stitch embroidered quilts and garments.
Phulkari (Punjab)

Floral embroidery with bright floss on odhnis/dupattas.
Kashida (Kashmir)

Crewel and sozni embroidery with nature motifs.
Kalamkari Textiles (AP/Telangana)

Hand-painted and block-printed sacred textiles.
