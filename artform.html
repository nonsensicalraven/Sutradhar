Art Forms

Painting and Scrolls

Indian painting spans from prehistoric rock art and Ajanta murals to Mughal–Rajput miniatures and living folk styles, unified by vivid color, symbolism, and storytelling. Scroll traditions narrate epics and local lore across long cloth or paper with bold outlines and earthy palettes.
Textile, Dyeing, and Printing Arts

Indian textiles excel in weaving, dyeing, printing, and embroidery, from fine cottons and silks to regionally distinct brocades, jamdani, and shawls. Resist-dye and print traditions—bandhani, leheriya, ajrakh, kalamkari, ikat—create signature motifs with natural dyes, hand-blocks, and yarn-dye techniques.
Ritual Floor and Body Arts

Ritual floor arts like rangoli, kolam, alpana, and mandana are ephemeral threshold designs made with rice flour or colored powders to invite auspiciousness, protection, and welcome. Body arts such as mehndi (henna), alta, and turmeric-based adornment mark rites and festivals with symbolic motifs that bless, beautify, and ward off evil.
Masks, Puppetry, and Performing Crafts

Masks in Indian performance traditions—such as Chhau, Theyyam, and Himalayan Cham—are crafted from clay, wood, bamboo, or papier-mâché to embody deities, spirits, and heroes in ritual theatre. Puppetry spans string (Kathputli), shadow (Tholu Bommalata, Togalu Gombeyaata), and glove/rod forms, with artisans making masks, costumes, and props that animate epics and folklore on stage.
Weaving and Loom Heritage

India’s weaving and loom heritage spans millennia, from fine cotton muslins and khadi to luxuriant silks like Kanchipuram, Banarasi, Paithani, and rich woollens such as Kani and Kullu shawls. Distinct regional looms and techniques create signature textures, borders, and motifs, preserving living traditions of handloom craftsmanship.
Stone, Lacquer, and Other Crafts

Stone crafts include temple sculpture, jali latticework, and everyday utensils carved in marble, sandstone, and granite. Lacquer crafts like Etikoppaka, Channapatna, and Kutch turn wood and gourds glossy for toys, bangles, and decor, while allied traditions—bidri inlay, bell-metal casting, terracotta, cane-bamboo, and wood carving—shape durable objects with simple, regional motifs.
